Singer’s Bio

Edward James Olmos was born to Mexican-American parents Pedro Olmos and Eleanor Huizar. Growing up in a predominantly Mexican neighborhood, he learned how to speak fluent Spanish, which later came in handy for his acting career. Olmos attended Montebello High School, where he developed an interest in music and drama. After graduating, he joined the United States Marine Corps and served for three years.

Career

Olmos began his acting career in the late 1970s, playing small roles in various TV shows and films. However, his breakthrough came in 1981 when he portrayed the role of Gaff in the cult classic Blade Runner. He followed this up with several notable performances in films such as Stand and Deliver, American Me, and Selena. In 1992, he starred in the television series, The Burning Season, which earned him an Emmy Award for Best Actor. Besides acting, Olmos has also ventured into producing and directing, with credits for American Me, Walkout, and The Plan.

Top Songs

Apart from acting, Olmos is also a talented singer. In 2004, he released his debut album, Olmos De Mexico, which blends traditional Mexican music with jazz, rock, and blues elements. The album features classic songs such as Guantanamera, La Bamba, and Alma Llanera. Some of his other noteworthy songs include Mujer, Los Laureles, and La Sandunga.
