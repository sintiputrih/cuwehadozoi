FBI: International, the third series in the FBI franchise, follows a team of FBI Special Agents who investigate crime and terrorism abroad. Although the showrunner Derek Haas told Parade that there are “93 [FBI] offices in embassies and military bases around the world” ahead of the series premiere, the team primarily works out of the United States Embassy in Budapest. The 2nd season of the CBS show is almost close to the midseason fall finale, and we recently saw the appearance of Ken Dandridge, played by Michael Torpey, who had not appeared on the series since the last episode of Season 1.

In this article below, let’s learn more about the role that Michael plays in FBI: International plus his acting career, net worth, personal life, and more.

Michael Torpey On FBI: International

In the CBS show, the team of FBI Special Agents meets Michael’s character, Ken Dandrige, at the United States Embassy in Budapest. Ken is the legal attache at the embassy and holds a quite bit of power.

According to the Internet, the legal attache is someone who is responsible for: coordinating requests for FBI or host country assistance overseas, conducting investigations in coordination with the host government; sharing investigative leads and information, briefing embassy counterparts from other agencies, including law enforcement agencies, as appropriate, and ambassadors, managing country clearances, and other jobs.

When Ken appears on the screen, he is asked to monitor The Fly Team and is put on the Hutchinson case. In the finale of FBI: International Season 1, Ken was on the top of the team while they were investigating the death of an American model in Milan. He wanted visibility in the case, which really got under Special Agent Jamie Kellet’s (played by Heida Reed) skin.

Michael Torpey Movies And TV Shows

Michael is an actor and writer by profession. He is best known for his performances on Orange Is The New Black (played by CO Thomas Humphrey), VEEP, and Inside Amy Schumer. Fans of VEEP will most likely remember him as she played Catherine’s boyfriend who kissed her onstage at the National Convention.

Michael has also had small roles on 30 Rock, The Eric Andre Show, and frequent appearances on Late Night with Jimmy Fallon. In The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel, he played the chauvinistic Eugene.

Some of Michael’s other acting credits include Sneaky Pete, Red Oaks, Thanksgiving, Net_Work, and The Awesomes, to name a few. According to his bio on IMDB, he has also appeared in numerous commercial campaigns such as Hanes (where he starred opposite NBA legend Michael Jordan), NAPA Auto Parts, Chase Bank, and Time Warner Cable.

The FBI: International actor has also created two failed pilots for Comedy Central.

Is Michael Torpey Married?

Michael Torpey is married to his wife, Alpana Choudhary. Reportedly, the two married on January 24, 2012.

Alpana is a therapist by profession. She founded Wove Therapy in 2018 with the aim of centering both clients and clinicians with marginalized identities. On January 10, 2022, Michael took to Facebook to share how he is “constantly impressed by his wife’s passion and drive to bring mental health services to communities that thought they were not for them.”

Prior to founding Wove Therapy, Alpana was privately practicing psychotherapy, incorporating several major therapeutic approaches into her practice such as Cognitive Behavior Therapy (CBT), Psychodynamic Psychotherapy, and Gestalt therapy, in New York City.

41-year-old Alpana completed her BA in Psychology from Barnard College and her MA in Counseling Psychology from New York University.

In 2019, Michael came up with the idea for TruTV’s Paid Off with Michael Torpey, where students compete in a trivia contest to earn money to repay their student loans when he found out that his wife had about $40,000 in college debt. The Inside Amy Schuamer actor told that he used the money he got from doing an underpants commercial to clear off his wife’s debt.

Michael and his wife Alpana also have one daughter, Eesha, from their marriage.
