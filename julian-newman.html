Julian Newman- Biography

Julian Newman is an amateur basketball player for his high school team, where he plays point guard. His father forced him to play in recreational leagues with boys older than him because he had a passion for basketball when he was a child. He has broken records and scored 3,873 total points as a consequence of his dedication and passion for the game. Despite being in seventh grade, he is the youngest player to collect 1000 career varsity points. He’s also the star of his documentary, “Born Ready.”

Julian Newman – Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Parents

On September 6th, 2001, he was born. His father Jamie Newman, mother Vivian Gonzalez, and younger sister Jaden make up his family. His mother was a point guard for University High School as well. She afterward served in the US Navy for four years before working for the US Postal Service.

Even in third grade, Jaden was a member of the varsity squad for Downey Christian High School, which received a lot of attention, and she appeared on shows like The Queen Latifah Show. Since then, his father has been preparing him for the game. He is currently enrolled in Prodigy Prep, a new school in Orlando founded by his father.

Julian Newman- Professional Career

We can only speak of his career as a player in elementary and high school because he is still a 19-year-old student in high school. He used to play for the middle school squad after starting at Downey Christian High School in 2012. He had rapid success and was capable of hitting 91 points in a game. As a result, he was elevated to the school’s varsity team. He averaged 2.4 points, 11 assists, and 4.3 steals per game in his first three games. Downey Christian had a 21-6 point record thanks to Newman while leading the state of Florida in assists.

On the 19th of December, 2012, he received a promotion to the next class, and his average score increased to 17 points per game. He was featured in a story titled “Fifth-grader starting for Florida varsity team” on the website MaxPreps. His highlight video, which was shared on YouTube by ScoutsFocus, went viral and has over 3.5 million views. The fact that Newman was only in fifth grade when he made the varsity team drew global news, notably in The New York Times and Sports Illustrated. He began making television appearances on shows such as Steve Harvey, The Ellen DeGeneres Show, Good Morning America, and others.

Fame

He was rewarded by being invited to sing during halftime at an Orlando Magic NBA game. In December 2013, The Huffington Post dubbed him “The Best Sixth Grade Basketball Player You’ve Ever Seen.” He averaged 13.6 points and 10.5 assists per game during that time. The Tampa Bay Times dubbed him “the world’s most promoted 12-year-old basketball player” the following year. While his game and rankings were praised, several media sites had polarized viewpoints and criticized the headlines by comparing him to a child star.

He set a record as the youngest player to amass 1000 career varsity points in seventh grade. After the 2014-15 season, he averaged 19.8 points, 10.2 assists, and 3.2 steals per game. Julian started starring in his documentary series, “Born Ready,” which was posted to YouTube by Elite Mixtapes in 2015. On the 4th of October, 2016, he scored 52 points in a widely publicized match-up with top 2020 class recruit Kyree Walker at the Middle School Hoops TV camp. Newman’s highlight reel from the game has surpassed 10 million views on YouTube.

He achieved a new Florida prep record of 3,873 points, surpassing Teddy Dupay’s mark of 3,873 points set in 1998. During the 2018-19 season, he averaged 34.7 points, seven assists, five rebounds, and four steals per game. And, as previously reported, he is currently enrolled at Prodigy Prep, which his father founded. Julian and his family were featured in the reality show “Hello Newmans,” which premiered on the sports network Overtime in 2019. He’s also a part of his family’s sportswear company, Prodigy.
