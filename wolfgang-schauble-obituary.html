Wolfgang Schuble Obituary

 Posted on February 28, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  290 words  |  Martina Birk

Wolfgang Schäuble, a stalwart German politician, minister, and statesman, whose remarkable political journey spanned over five decades, bid farewell on December 26, 2023, succumbing to cancer at the age of 81, surrounded by his family.

Born in Freiburg in 1942, Schäuble emerged as a leading figure within the conservative Christian Democrats (CDU). His political odyssey began in 1972 when he entered parliament, and he went on to secure victory in his Offenburg constituency in every subsequent election, earning the distinction of being Germany’s longest-serving postwar parliamentarian.

Internationally renowned, Schäuble gained prominence during the aftermath of the 2008 financial crisis and the ensuing challenges in the eurozone sovereign debt.

Serving as Germany’s fiscally hawkish finance minister, he advocated for stringent fiscal policies, urging southern European countries to curtail their borrowing. This period also marked Schäuble’s polarizing presence, particularly in countries like Greece.

At a juncture, Schäuble seemed poised to ascend to Germany’s top political position, following in the footsteps of Chancellor Helmut Kohl. His political repertoire included roles as the interior minister, a position he held in 1990 when an assassination attempt left him with gunshot wounds, confining him to a wheelchair for the remainder of his life.

Survived by his wife Ingeborg and four children, Schäuble’s passing reverberated across the political spectrum. Friedrich Merz, the current CDU leader, expressed profound sadness, describing Schäuble as his closest friend and confidant in politics. Merz’s sentiments, shared on social media, reflected the deep impact Schäuble had on those within his political orbit.

As Germany mourns the loss of a political luminary, Wolfgang Schäuble’s legacy endures—a testament to his enduring commitment to public service, resilience in the face of adversity, and the indelible mark he left on the nation and the international political stage.
