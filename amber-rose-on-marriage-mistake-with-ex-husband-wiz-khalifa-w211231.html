Amber Rose on Marriage Mistake With Ex-Husband Wiz Khalifa

 Posted on April 2, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  353 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Oh, what could have been. Amber Rose revealed in a new interview with Bethenny Frankel that she believes that she and her ex-husband, Wiz Khalifa, would still be together if they’d gotten married later in life.

"I feel like that we just rushed into marriage because we were so deeply in love, and we kind of had a rough patch in our marriage,” the Amber Rose Show host, 32, explained on the Wednesday, June 22, edition of SiriusXM’s B Real With Bethenny. “And because we were married, it kind of, like, brought it to the next level."

"I feel like if we just stayed in just a boyfriend-girlfriend relationship longer, we could have worked it out and then got married later,” she added.

The former couple wed in 2013 after two years of dating and now share custody of their 3-year-old son, Sebastian. Although Rose filed for divorce from the “See You Again” rapper, 28, after 14 months of marriage (she cited irreconcilable differences), the pair have settled their differences and are closer than ever.

"It's so crazy that we are literally the best of friends," Rose revealed. "We hang out all the time. We have full family days with the baby."

"We just went out to a strip club the other night together — without the baby. Just me and Wiz," she added. "We just had a great f–king time. We spent like [$30,000] on strippers. It was sick. It was so cool."

Although the Real Housewives of New York City star suggested that Rose might one day rekindle her romance with Khalifa, the How to Be a Bad Bitch author doesn’t want to rush back into anything.

"I just feel like right now he should go be a ho for a while and work on his businesses, and then maybe later we can get back," she responded. "If God wants us together, we will be together."

Most recently, the “Slut Walk” founder has been linked to Toronto Raptors basketball player Terrence Ross. Prior to that, she famously dated Kanye West from 2009 until 2010.
