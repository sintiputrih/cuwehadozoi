College Football Playoff Expansion: Implications for Teams and Fans

 Posted on August 19, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  511 words  |  Martina Birk

The College Football Playoff (CFP) is poised to expand — for a long time — to upheave the college football landscape, with wide-ranging implications for teams, fans, and the sport. With the playoff session stretching out to no more than 12 contestants beginning in 2024-25 — and possibly 14 members in future seasons, if approved — this has gotten talk of being about the playoffs. In this post, we are going to dive into the fins and take a detailed look at what this major change means for college football.


Greater Accessibility Team Applications


The CFP's growth presents a grand chance — to take as many athletic departments with it in the direction of finally having financially feasible models for the athlete compensation that looms. That was more than an expanded playoff would revolutionize college sports and revenue earning potential that could then be used to bolster athletic programs and compensate athletes. On the other hand, the expansion also leads to doubts in terms of what this means for teams and conferences, in regards to their admission in a conference and profit sharing.


Team Selection and Seeding


Changes to how teams are selected and seeded are among the most notable in the wake of the expansion. Now, with five conference champions and seven at-large teams (12 teams total) making the playoff, the formula for selecting and seeding teams is much more multifaceted. This then could make the seeding of the teams up in the air, with automatic bids for conference champions and potential upsets in conference championship games.


Financial Implications


There are financial consequences to the added playoff round. The long-expected TV contract for the expanded playoff is projected to be the largest in college sports history, a potential influx of money that the governing body estimates could exceed $2 billion annually. However, the revenue bump from the expanded playoff gives a chance for athletics programs to be supported and not to face the expense of compensating athletes, shaping the future of college sports financially.


Impact on Fans

The new format is going to bring a more dynamic and equitable playoff experience, with more teams than ever competing for the national championship. But it also comes with practical hurdles, as fans will need to get oriented to the new setup and be able to grasp the rankings and seeding.


Viewing Experience


The expanded playoff is can be great, with both a wider variety of national championship matchups and more games that would, TV execs presume, be a hit. However the growing complexity of the playoff system means that the broader audience must be properly apprised of the process and how teams are chosen, suggest broadcast, and graphics optimization as a result.


In the End

While the consequences are wide for teams and fans, the final effect appears to be a big factor. For better or worse, the expanded playoff will have major consequences on the college football landscape, from financial implications to the way the sport is watched and experienced and the role of the fan in the modern game.
